Salakovskite lakes

The Salakovo Lakes are two glacial lakes on the Jakupica Plateau in Macedonia, located at an altitude of 2180 m above sea level. They are two rivers, which are the only lakes on this massif. They are called Salakovski Lakes (Great and Small Salakovo Lake), and are formed in the Ezera locality in the northeastern parts of Karadzica below the Ubava peak.

Solunska

The Jakupica mountain range (Solunska Glava, 2,540 m), which covers the central part of the Republic of Macedonia, with an area above 1,000 m above sea level (1,262 km2) surpasses all higher mountains. It is a striking massif with several levels, interconnected morphologically, and most of all structurally connected into one whole.

Explore The Jakupica

Jakupica or Mokra Planina — a mountain range in central Macedonia. It consists of several mountain parts: Karadžica, Dautica, Golešnica, Kitka, Mumdžica, Salakova Planina and Osoj. The mountain has heavy traces of the ice age, represented by cirques (two lakes), waves and moraines. Jakupica is rich in diverse flora and fauna, pastures, springs - hot springs and others. The highest and most notable peak is Solunska Glava, 2,540 meters high, one of the most interesting places is the Dolna Babina Dupka area with the Nežilovskite Steni.
